About

Temple Sinai is an, egalitarian, Conservative synagogue in Burlington County, New Jersey committed to the following:

  • Hachnasat Orchim: We are a warm and accepting congregation devoted to diversity where we respect and honor our members for who they are.

  • L'dor V'dor: We provide a comfortable environment dedicated to the continuity of Jewish life through education, study of Torah, worship, and the celebration of Jewish customs and traditions.

  • Kehillah: We serve the Jewish community, the wider community, and the State of Israel though acts of Tikuum Olam (repairing the world), and G'milut Chasadim (acts of living kindness) to those in need.

Services

Temple Sinai conducts weekly Friday night and Saturday morning, Shabbat services. Services are generally at 7:00 p.m. on Friday evening and 10 a.m. on Saturday morning. It is always a good idea to check the "Happenings" or call the office (856 829-0658) to check the Service schedule recording. Service times can also be found on our website calendar.

Most months a Shabbat Service is designated as a Family Service. This service is oriented toward the children and includes participation from the children in our Religious School, who are expected to attend. Consult the Bulletin for dates and times.

Every other month Temple Sinai holds a Tot Shabbat. This lively interactive Friday night service is especially for young children (six years old and younger) and their families. During a Tot Shabbat service, we celebrate the beauty and wonder of Shabbat through singing, dancing, and story telling, as well as teach basic Shabbat prayers, songs and rituals. Oneg Shabbat refreshments follow the service. Tot Shabbat is a wonderful way to introduce your toddler or preschooler to Jewish worship and to other children in the Temple Sinai community in a relaxed atmosphere. The Tot Shabbat is held at 7:00 pm and usually lasts 30 to 45 minutes. Please refer to the Bulletin for dates.

Services for the High Holidays are held on Slichot night, Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur. Morning services begin at 9:30 am and are held in the Main Sanctuary, led by Rabbi Reaboi. A Family Service for children and their parents lasts about 1 hour and is held in the chapel at 9:30 a.m. Refreshments are available for the children. Call 856 829-0658 for more information. Congregants will receive High Holiday tickets, with service schedules, in the mail. Tickets are required for admission into the main High Holiday Services for security reasons, and are included as part of your dues. Seating is on a first come basis.

Torah Spark

Come join Rabbi Reaboi on Saturday’s at 10am for our weekly Torah spark, where we read through the weekly Torah portion and discuss it from several different perspectives: theological, cultural, political, and communal. No prior knowledge is required. Just come ready to learn and engage with the text and your fellow congregants.

Special note: Weekly Torah sparks are typically postponed when we are having traditional Saturday morning services or holiday programming.
